Stitches needed

All these stitches are in US crochet terms. The only stiches you need to make this blanket are:

  • Chain stitch (CH) - Every square starts with a row of chain stitches, this is not included when counting the rows. At the end of each row you will chain 1 and turn, this chain doesn't count as a stitch.
  • Single crochet (SC) - This is the majority of the blanket. Whenever I say stitch, this is what I mean.
  • single crochet two together (SC2TG) - You only need this stitch for attaching the pokemon squares to the element squares.

Sewing in the ends

As you crochet, crochet in the end strands. This will take care of some work sewing it all together, but you will still have a fair amount of sewing!

The gaps between the pokemon

Each element square is 25 stitches wide and tall (rows), and each pokemon square is 65 stitches wide and tall. With that information, each border between the pokemon squares needs to 25x65 stitches. The gaps on the outside are made of only red. The gaps on the inside, around Eevee are special.

For the inner gaps, you are stitching squares in the colours of pokeballs. The ones above and below Eevee are 25 stitches tall and 20 wide: 10 rows of red, 5 rows od black and 10 rows of white. The ones on either side of Eevee are 20 stitches tall and 25 wide: 8 rows of red, 4 rows od black and 8 rows of white. You need to make 8 of each special square.

Once you have a pokemon square finished and the element square that goes with it, you can stitch them together (stitch not sew). Orient the square so the element is in the corner that will be closest to Eevee. Starting on the other end of the square, stitch along until the last stitch. In that stitch, you SC2TG with the element square stitch and turn your work. The next stitch is SC2TG, but you start in the next element stitch and turn so the stitch finishes in the last stitch you did on the last row. For every corner square, you should have the element and 2 red borders attached.

Sewing it together

You should have the four corners (pokemon with element and 2 red borders attached), 2 middle pieces (pokemon with red border underneath), Eevee, 4 unused elements (that match the middle pokemon), 4 pokeball colour borders (call this pokeball-A 25 stitches tall x 20 rows) and 4 pokeball borders (call this pokeball-B 20 stitches tall x 25 rows). Staring with the unused elements and pokeball coloured borders, sew the electric element between two pokeball-A pieces, repeat this for ice element. Sew the dark element between two pokeball-B pieces, repeat this for the psychic element. sew these four gap pieces to the Eevee. Electric on top, dark on the left, ice on the bottom and psychic on the right. You should now have 4 corner pieces, 1 center and 4 middle pieces. These can all be sewn together following the picture at the top of the 'Patterns' page.

Yarn colours

The following table makes figuring out the yarn colours for the pokemon easy (there are a lot). I suggest only having the colours you need for each square out at once, so you don't get everything mixed up.

For the element squares, the colours are

  • Water - White, bright blue and light blue
  • Electric - White, gold and bright yellow
  • Fire - White, orange and reddish-orange
  • Psychic - White, light purple and purple
  • Dark - White, black and grey
  • Grass - White, black, light green and green
  • Ice - White and frost blue
  • Fairy - White, bright pink and dark pink
